Why Claims Are Stressful

Filing A Roof Claim Isn't Something Most People Do Twice

Most homeowners file a roof claim once, maybe twice, in their lifetime. The forms are confusing, the deadlines are tight, and you're expected to describe damage you can't even see from the ground. It's an uneven fight: the insurance company handles claims every day, and you're doing it for the first time under pressure.

Adjusters move fast and they don't always catch everything. A missed section of hail damage or an underestimated scope of work means you settle for less than the repair actually costs, and you're the one left covering the gap. We've walked hundreds of homeowners through this exact process. We work for you, not the insurance company, and we don't leave money on the table.

Understanding Your Deductible

What You'll Actually Pay Out Of Pocket

Your deductible is the amount you're responsible for before your insurance coverage kicks in, and for wind and hail damage, most Texas and Oklahoma policies handle it a little differently than a standard homeowners deductible.

A lot of policies in this region carry a separate wind and hail deductible, often a percentage of your home's insured value rather than a flat dollar amount. That number is set in your policy, not by us and not by your adjuster. Once a claim is approved, your insurer pays the covered cost of repair or replacement, minus that deductible. On a properly approved claim, that deductible is typically the only out-of-pocket cost you'll see, and we'll walk you through exactly what yours looks like before any work starts.
